01

Who We Are

Dilorra Marine is a marine logistics and shipping company incorporated in Canada, with its head office at 577 Marginal Rd, Halifax, NS B3H 4P6 and a regional office at 16 Bath Rd, Kingston, ON K7L 1H4.

Dilorra Marine is the "data controller" for the personal information you provide to us through our website, quote requests, service agreements, and any other interactions. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle that information in accordance with the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) and applicable provincial privacy legislation.

If you have any questions about how we handle your personal information, please contact us at info@dilorramarine.com before using our services.
02

Information We Collect

We collect personal information only when it is necessary to provide our services or to respond to your inquiries. The categories of information we may collect include:

Information You Provide Directly:

  • Name, job title, and company name.
  • Email address and telephone number.
  • Postal/business address.
  • Cargo details, shipping requirements, and freight specifications.
  • Payment and billing information (processed securely via third-party payment processors).
  • Messages, inquiries, or correspondence submitted via our contact form or email.

Information Collected Automatically:

  • IP address and browser type.
  • Device and operating system information.
  • Pages visited, time spent on pages, and referring URLs.
  • Cookie identifiers and session data (see Section 6).

Information from Third Parties:

  • Business contact information from carriers, agents, or logistics partners in the course of arranging shipments.
  • Publicly available business information for due diligence purposes.
03

How We Use Your Information

We use the personal information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Service Delivery – To provide, manage, and coordinate shipping, logistics, and freight services.
  • Quote & Contract Processing – To respond to quote requests, prepare service agreements, and issue invoices.
  • Communications – To respond to your inquiries, send operational updates, and provide customer support.
  • Legal & Regulatory Compliance – To fulfil customs, tax, import/export documentation, and other legal obligations.
  • Safety & Security – To verify identities, prevent fraud, and protect our business and clients.
  • Website Improvement – To analyse website usage, improve user experience, and maintain website security.
  • Marketing – To send relevant updates about our services, where you have provided consent or where permitted by law. You may opt out at any time.

We will not use your personal information for any purpose incompatible with those for which it was collected without your prior consent.

04

Legal Basis for Processing

Under Canadian privacy law, we rely on the following lawful bases for processing your personal information:

  • Consent – Where you have given us clear consent to process your data for a specific purpose (e.g., marketing communications).
  • Contract – Where processing is necessary to fulfil a contract with you, or to take steps at your request before entering into one.
  • Legal Obligation – Where processing is necessary to comply with applicable Canadian law (e.g., customs reporting, tax obligations).
  • Legitimate Interests – Where processing is necessary for our legitimate business interests (e.g., improving our services, fraud prevention), provided these interests are not overridden by your rights.
05

Sharing Your Information

Dilorra Marine does not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share your information in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service Partners & Subcontractors – Carriers, port agents, customs brokers, warehousing providers, and other logistics partners required to perform our services on your behalf. These parties are contractually bound to handle your data securely and only as instructed.
  • Payment Processors – Secure third-party payment platforms for billing and invoicing purposes.
  • IT & Software Providers – Cloud hosting, CRM, and email service providers supporting our operations, subject to data processing agreements.
  • Legal & Regulatory Authorities – Government agencies, customs authorities, or courts where required by law or to protect our legal rights.
  • Business Transfers – In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to equivalent privacy protections.

We do not transfer personal information outside of Canada without ensuring appropriate safeguards are in place (see Section 9).

06

Cookies & Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your browsing experience and analyse website performance. Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit our site.

Types of cookies we use:

  • Essential Cookies – Required for the website to function correctly. These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics Cookies – Used to understand how visitors interact with our site (e.g., Google Analytics). Data is aggregated and anonymised.
  • Preference Cookies – Store your settings and preferences to improve your experience on return visits.
You can control or disable cookies through your browser settings. Disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website. By continuing to use our site, you consent to our use of cookies as described above.

We do not use cookies for targeted advertising or third-party ad networks.

07

Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, including legal, accounting, and reporting requirements.

  • Client Records & Service Data – Retained for a minimum of 7 years following the end of a business relationship, in line with Canadian tax and commercial record-keeping obligations.
  • Customs & Shipping Documentation – Retained in accordance with applicable customs and trade regulations (typically 6–7 years).
  • Marketing Contact Data – Retained until you withdraw consent or request deletion.
  • Website Analytics Data – Retained in anonymised/aggregated form for up to 26 months.

When personal information is no longer required, we securely delete or anonymise it.

08

Data Security

Dilorra Marine takes reasonable and appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal information against unauthorised access, disclosure, loss, or destruction. These include:

  • Encrypted data transmission (HTTPS/TLS) across our website and systems.
  • Access controls and role-based permissions for staff handling personal data.
  • Regular security assessments and staff training.
  • Secure, password-protected systems with multi-factor authentication where applicable.

While we take all reasonable precautions, no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. In the event of a data breach that poses a risk of significant harm, we will notify affected individuals and the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada as required by law.

09

International Data Transfers

As a global marine logistics company operating across 40+ countries, some of your personal information may be transferred to, stored in, or processed in countries outside Canada in connection with the delivery of our services (e.g., data shared with overseas port agents or carriers).

Where personal information is transferred internationally, we ensure adequate protections are in place, such as contractual clauses requiring the recipient to provide comparable levels of protection. By using our services, you acknowledge that your information may be processed in countries that may have different privacy laws than Canada.

10

Your Privacy Rights

Under PIPEDA and applicable Canadian privacy law, you have the following rights in respect of your personal information:

Right of Access

Request a copy of the personal information we hold about you and how it is used.

Right to Correction

Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal information.

Right to Withdrawal of Consent

Withdraw consent for processing where it is based on consent (e.g., marketing), subject to legal or contractual limitations.

Right to Deletion

Request deletion of your personal information where we no longer have a lawful reason to retain it.

Right to Lodge a Complaint

File a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada if you believe your privacy rights have been violated.

Right to Object

Object to certain types of processing, including direct marketing communications.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at info@dilorramarine.com. We will respond to all requests within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before processing a request.

11

Children's Privacy

Our website and services are intended for business use and are directed at adults (18 years of age and older). We do not knowingly collect personal information from individuals under the age of 18. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected personal information from a minor, we will delete it promptly.

If you believe we have collected personal information from a child, please contact us immediately at info@dilorramarine.com.

12

Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites, carrier portals, or external resources. These sites operate independently and are not governed by this Privacy Policy. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party sites you visit. Dilorra Marine is not responsible for the privacy practices or content of external sites.

13

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, or legal requirements. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, notify you by email.

Your continued use of our website or services after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated Privacy Policy. We encourage you to review this page periodically to stay informed.
